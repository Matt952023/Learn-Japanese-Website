Lessons

  • Hiragana Vowels — a / i / u / e / o

    The first five characters in Hirigana is あ, い, う, え, and お. These characters are important because they establish the five basic vowels of Japanese. Almost every other hirigana combines one consonant and one of these five vowels.

    あ (a)

    Romaji: a

    Pronunciation: like the “a” in “father” (ah).

    Use examples:

    • あさasa – morning
    • あめame – rain or candy (different kanji)

    Often appears at the start of many beginner words and basic phrases.

    い (i)

    Romaji: i

    Pronunciation: like “ee” in “see”.

    Use examples:

    • いぬinu – dog
    • いえie – house

    Shows up in many adjectives ending in 〜い (like たかい = tall/expensive).

    う (u)

    Romaji: u

    Pronunciation: like “oo” in “food”, but often very short and sometimes almost whispered in casual speech.

    Use examples:

    • うみumi – sea
    • うたuta – song

    Also used in verb endings like 〜う, 〜く, 〜す (dictionary forms).

    え (e)

    Romaji: e

    Pronunciation: like “e” in “get” (not like “ee”).

    Use examples:

    • えきeki – station
    • えんぴつenpitsu – pencil

    Shows up in many basic nouns and particles (like へ which is pronounced “e” when used as a particle).

    お (o)

    Romaji: o

    Pronunciation: like “o” in “orange” but without the “w” glide (more like a pure “oh”).

    Use examples:

    • おかねokane – money
    • おちゃocha – tea

    Often appears in polite words starting with お〜 as an honorific prefix (like おちゃ, おなまえ).
